# CH03 – The Foster Room Wasn't Home, But God Was

**Scripture:** Psalm 27:10 — "When my father and my mother forsake me, then the Lord will take me up."
**Theme:** Foster care, belonging, God's presence when the room wasn't home
**Status:** Needs Loretta Review

---

## Chapter Body

I do not have many pictures from my young life.

Foster care did not leave me with photo albums. It did not leave me with boxes full of childhood memories, birthday pictures, family vacations, or easy proof of where I belonged. There are parts of my early story that live more in my spirit than in photographs.

But just because there are no pictures does not mean God was not there.

There were rooms I stayed in that did not feel like home. There were places where my body was present, but my heart was still searching for belonging. A child can sit on a bed, look around a room, and know that the walls around her are not the same as being covered, chosen, or rooted.

But Psalm 27:10 says, "When my father and my mother forsake me, then the Lord will take me up."

That scripture became more than words. It became a witness.

God saw the little girl in the room. He saw what I could not explain. He saw the questions I did not have language for. He saw the loneliness, the moving, the waiting, the wondering, and the quiet places where I needed someone to say, "You belong."

And even when the room was not home, God was there.

I may not have all the childhood pictures, but I have testimony. I may not have albums from every season, but I have evidence in my spirit that the Lord kept me. He took me up when life did not hold me the way I needed to be held.

This chapter is not written to make my childhood look easy.

It is written to say that God was present in the rooms that did not feel like home.

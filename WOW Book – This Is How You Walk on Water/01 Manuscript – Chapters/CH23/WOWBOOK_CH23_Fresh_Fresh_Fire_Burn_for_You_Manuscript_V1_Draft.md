# CH23 – Fresh Fresh Fire: Burn for You

**Scripture:** Acts 2:3–4 — "And there appeared unto them cloven tongues like as of fire… And they were all filled with the Holy Ghost…"
**Theme:** Fresh fire, Holy Spirit, purification, passion for God, renewal, surrender
**Status:** Pending Loretta Approval

---

## Chapter Body

After everything I have been through, I still want to burn for God.

Not burn out.

Burn for Him.

There is a difference.

Burnout comes from carrying what God never told me to carry. Holy fire comes from surrendering myself back to Him and saying, "Lord, fill me again."

There were seasons where pain tried to put my fire out. Sickness tried to put it out. Betrayal tried to put it out. Grief tried to put it out. Weariness tried to make my spirit tired, my worship quiet, and my yes heavy.

But somewhere down in my soul, there was still a cry:

Fresh fresh fire.

Burn for You.

Acts 2 says they were filled with the Holy Ghost, and fire appeared among them. That fire was not there to destroy them. It was there to fill them, mark them, empower them, and send them.

That is the kind of fire I need.

Fire that purifies, but does not consume me.

Fire that strengthens, but does not harden me.

Fire that burns away fear, bitterness, pride, weariness, and anything that tries to stand between me and God.

I do not want to just survive my testimony and lose my fire.

I do not want to come out of pain cold.

I do not want to go through deep waters and stop burning for the One who kept me.

So I ask Him again:

Lord, give me fresh fire.

Not yesterday's oil.

Not old strength.

Not a borrowed flame.

Fresh fire.

Fire for my assignment.

Fire for my prayer life.

Fire for my worship.

Fire for my writing.

Fire for the nations.

Fire for every daughter and son I am called to cover.

Fire for the book.

Fire for the testimony.

Fire to keep saying yes when my body is tired and my heart has been tested.

This fire is sacred.

This fire is not performance.

This fire is not for applause.

This fire is for God.

After all I have survived, I still want my life to say, "Lord, I burn for You."

## Reader Takeaway
Do not confuse burnout with holy fire. God can reignite what pain tried to put out and fill you again with fresh passion for Him.

## Prayer
Holy Spirit, fill me again. Burn away what is not like You. Restore the fire that pain, grief, sickness, and weariness tried to put out. Give me fresh hunger, fresh strength, fresh worship, and fresh surrender. Let my life burn for You without burning out. Mark me with holy fire for my assignment, my family, my healing, and the nations You have called me to reach. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
I will burn for God, not burn out.
Fresh fire is falling on my life.
The Holy Spirit is renewing my yes.
Pain did not put out my flame.
My life belongs to God's fire.

## 🔒 Production Directions (internal — not printed unless Loretta says)

**Protected Testimony Summary**
Asking God for fresh fire after long seasons of pain, weariness, sickness, loss, and warfare — the cry of a woman who has been through the water, the shadows, the hospital room, the valley, but still says, "Lord, let me burn for You." Not destructive fire — holy fire of the Holy Spirit that purifies, renews, strengthens, and revives.

**Where God Was**
God was in the burning. God was in the renewal. God was in the fire that did not destroy. God was in the fresh hunger to keep saying yes. God was reigniting what pain tried to put out.

**Privacy Boundaries**
- Do not make the fire look destructive, dangerous, or frightening.
- Do not show the woman burning or harmed.
- Do not make it look like chaos.
- Keep the fire holy, soft, golden, sacred, and Spirit-led.
- Keep the tone worshipful, surrendered, and empowering.

**Visual Direction**
A graceful Black woman in prayer or worship, surrounded by soft golden holy fire that feels peaceful, sacred, and empowering. The fire should look like the presence of the Holy Spirit, not destruction. Use cream, gold, navy, and water-toned accents. The woman should look renewed, surrendered, and strong.

**Video Direction**
Open with dim embers, then golden holy fire gently rising around the woman as she prays. The mood should be worshipful and renewing, not intense or scary. End with her lifted face and the line: "I will burn for God, not burn out."

**Voiceover starter**
After everything I have been through, I still want to burn for God. Not burn out — burn for Him. Lord, give me fresh fresh fire.

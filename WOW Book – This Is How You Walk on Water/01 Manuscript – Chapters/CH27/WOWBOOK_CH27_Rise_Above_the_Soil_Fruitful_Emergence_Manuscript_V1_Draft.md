# CH27 – Rise Above the Soil: Fruitful Emergence

**Scripture:** John 12:24 — "Except a corn of wheat fall into the ground and die, it abideth alone: but if it die, it bringeth forth much fruit."
**Theme:** Burial seasons, hidden growth, resurrection, fruitfulness, becoming more after being planted
**Status:** Pending Loretta Approval

---

## Chapter Body

There are seasons where you feel buried.

Covered.

Hidden.

Pressed down.

Like everything around you is dirt, and nobody can see what God is doing underneath.

I know what that feels like.

There were times in my life when I did not feel seen. I did not feel celebrated. I did not feel like I was rising. I felt covered by pressure, pain, sickness, loss, and waiting. But what I thought was burial, God was using as planting.

John 12:24 says, "Except a corn of wheat fall into the ground and die, it abideth alone: but if it die, it bringeth forth much fruit."

That scripture teaches me that some fruit only comes after a seed goes into the ground.

A seed does not look like it is winning when it is buried.

It looks hidden.

It looks forgotten.

It looks covered.

But underneath the soil, something is happening.

Roots are forming.

Life is moving.

Purpose is being prepared.

And in the right season, what was hidden begins to break through.

I had to learn that God was not wasting my hidden seasons. He was not ignoring me in the dirt. He was not punishing me because I could not see fruit yet. He was producing something in me that needed roots before it could rise.

The soil did not stop me.

It strengthened me.

The pressure did not end me.

It prepared me.

The hidden place did not erase me.

It developed me.

This is why I can say I am rising above the soil. Not because I was never buried. Not because the dirt was not real. But because God put life in the seed.

What tried to bury me had to watch me become fruitful.

What covered me had to witness me emerge.

What looked like death became a place of multiplication.

I am not just coming up.

I am coming up fruitful.

## Reader Takeaway
Every hidden season is not punishment. Sometimes God plants you before He raises you, because fruit needs roots.

## Prayer
Lord, thank You for being with me in the hidden places. Thank You for producing fruit even when I felt buried. Teach me to trust the process of roots, waiting, and emergence. Let what pressed me become preparation. Let what covered me become testimony. Bring forth fruit from every season I thought was lost. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
I am rising above the soil.
What felt like burial was planting.
God is producing fruit from my hidden season.
The pressure prepared me.
I am coming up fruitful.

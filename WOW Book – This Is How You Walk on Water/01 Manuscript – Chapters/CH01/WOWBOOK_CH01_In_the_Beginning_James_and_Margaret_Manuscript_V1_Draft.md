# CH01 – In the Beginning: James and Margaret

**Scripture:** Jeremiah 1:5 — "Before I formed thee in the belly I knew thee…"
**Theme:** Origins & Foreknowledge
**Identity:** Known by God before the beginning
**Status:** Approved (Loretta) — 2026-07-03

---

## Chapter Body

Before I knew my own name, God knew me.

Before I understood family, pain, rejection, love, loss, or purpose, Heaven had already written something over my life. Jeremiah 1:5 says, "Before I formed thee in the belly I knew thee…" That scripture tells me my beginning did not start with people's opinions, people's failures, or even people's ability to love me correctly. My beginning started in God.

James and Margaret are part of where my story begins. Their names are connected to my roots, my history, and the doorway of this testimony. This chapter is not about making everything perfect. It is about honoring the beginning and recognizing that even in the places I did not fully understand, God was already present.

There are parts of a life that only make sense when you look back and see the hand of God. Some things I survived before I had language for them. Some things I carried before I knew how heavy they were. But now I can look back and say, "Lord, You were there."

This is where the walk begins.

Not because everything was easy.

Not because every person stayed.

Not because every wound was explained.

But because before I was formed, God knew me. Before I walked through water, He had already called me to come forth. Before I became the testimony, I was already His.

## Reader Takeaway
Your life did not begin with pain.
It began with God's knowledge of you.

## Declaration
I was known by God before I was known by people.
My beginning belongs to God.
My roots are being healed by the Lord.
Nothing in my story was hidden from Heaven.
I am not an accident; I am called.

## Prayer
> _Draft from Loretta's Prayer Direction — approve or edit._

Father, thank You for knowing me before my beginning. Before I had a name, before I had breath, before anyone could love me or fail me, You already knew me and called me Yours. Thank You that my story did not start in pain — it started in You.

Lord, heal the roots of my story. Touch the places I carried before I had words for them, the things I survived before I understood them. Where there was loss, bring Your presence. Where there was rejection, remind me I was chosen. Help me look back and see Your hand in every place I did not fully understand, and say with peace, "Lord, You were there."

I thank You that I am not an accident. I am known, I am called, and my beginning belongs to You. In Jesus' name, Amen.

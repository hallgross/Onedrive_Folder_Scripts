# CH11 – The Hospital Stay: God Was in the Room

**Scripture:** Psalm 46:1 — "God is our refuge and strength, a very present help in trouble."
**Theme:** Hospital stay; God present in weakness; healing beyond the physical
**Status:** Approved (Loretta) — 2026-07-03

---

## Chapter Body

There are some places you do not forget.

A hospital room is one of them.

The sounds. The lights. The bed. The waiting. The way your body feels when it is tired, and the way your spirit has to keep reaching for God even when your strength feels low.

I know what it feels like to be in a hospital bed and still have to believe God.

I know what it feels like to look around a room and realize that healing is not just physical. Sometimes healing is emotional. Sometimes it is spiritual. Sometimes it is the grace to breathe through another night, trust through another test, and believe through another report.

But God was in the room.

He was not far from me because I was weak. He did not step away because my body was going through something. He did not measure me by tubes, tests, pain, or what the doctors were watching.

Psalm 46:1 says, "God is our refuge and strength, a very present help in trouble."

Very present.

That means He was not absent. He was not late. He was not standing outside the door waiting for me to get strong enough to deserve Him.

He was present in the trouble.

He was present in the weakness.

He was present in the room.

There were moments when I did not have a long prayer. Sometimes all I had was, "Lord, help me." Sometimes all I had was a tear. Sometimes all I had was enough faith to look up.

And He met me there.

The hospital room became more than a place of sickness. It became a place where I learned that God can visit you in the middle of what feels clinical, cold, and uncertain. He can turn a room of machines into a room of mercy. He can turn a bed of weakness into an altar of trust.

I am not here because my body never went through anything.

I am here because God was with me while it did.

The hospital stay did not get the final word.

God was in the room.

## Reader Takeaway
God does not wait outside the door until you are strong enough. He is a very present help — with you in the weakness, in the waiting, and in the room.

## Prayer
Lord, thank You for being present in the room when I was weak. Thank You for meeting me in the short prayers, the tears, and the moments I could only look up. Turn every room of machines into a room of mercy, and every bed of weakness into an altar of trust. Heal what is physical, emotional, and spiritual. Let every reader in a hard place know You are a very present help in trouble. In Jesus' name, amen.

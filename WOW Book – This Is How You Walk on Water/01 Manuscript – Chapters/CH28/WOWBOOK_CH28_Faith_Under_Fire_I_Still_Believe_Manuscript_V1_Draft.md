# CH28 – Faith Under Fire: I Still Believe

**Scripture:** Daniel 3:17–18 — "Our God whom we serve is able to deliver us… But if not, be it known unto thee… we will not serve thy gods…"
**Theme:** Faith tested by fire, endurance, pressure, worship under trial, believing God even before deliverance
**Status:** Pending Loretta Approval

---

## Chapter Body

There is a kind of faith that is easy to talk about before the fire.

Before the diagnosis.

Before the betrayal.

Before the loss.

Before the waiting.

Before the pain keeps coming.

Before the report shakes you.

Before life asks you whether you still believe what you said you believed.

I know what it feels like to have faith under fire.

There were seasons where everything in me had to decide if I still believed God when the situation did not look like deliverance yet. I had to believe Him when my body was tired. I had to believe Him when my heart was hurting. I had to believe Him when money was funny, when people walked away, when reports came, when grief visited, and when I could not see how it was all going to work together.

But I still believe.

Daniel 3 tells the story of the three Hebrew boys facing the fiery furnace. They said God was able to deliver them. But they also said, "But if not…" they would still not bow.

That kind of faith touches me.

Because faith under fire is not only believing God can bring you out. Sometimes faith under fire is refusing to bow while you are still in it.

Refusing to bow to fear.

Refusing to bow to bitterness.

Refusing to bow to sickness as your identity.

Refusing to bow to rejection.

Refusing to bow to the report.

Refusing to bow to what the enemy said would be the end.

The fire was real.

But so was the fourth Man in the fire.

God was not waiting outside the flames for me to come out. He was with me in the middle of them. He was keeping what the fire wanted to consume. He was preserving my yes. He was protecting my worship. He was reminding me that even under pressure, I still belonged to Him.

I still believe.

Not because I have never been shaken.

Not because I have never cried.

Not because every question has been answered.

But because God has been faithful in every fire.

The fire did not make me bow.

The fire made me know Him deeper.

I was not just chosen to survive.

I was chosen to know Him.

## Reader Takeaway
Faith under fire is not pretending the flames are not real. It is refusing to bow to fear while God stands with you in the middle of them.

## Prayer
Lord, strengthen my faith in the fire. When pressure comes, help me not to bow to fear, bitterness, sickness, rejection, or despair. Remind me that You are able to deliver, and even while I wait, You are with me. Preserve my worship, protect my yes, and let my faith come out stronger than the flames that tested it. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
I still believe.
The fire will not make me bow.
God is with me in the flames.
My faith is stronger than fear.
I was chosen to know Him in the fire.

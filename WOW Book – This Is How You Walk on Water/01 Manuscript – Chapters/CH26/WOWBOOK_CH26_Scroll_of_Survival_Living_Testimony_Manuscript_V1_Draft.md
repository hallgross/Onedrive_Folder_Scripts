# CH26 – The Scroll of Survival: Living Testimony

**Scripture:** Malachi 3:16 — "...and a book of remembrance was written before him for them that feared the Lord…"
**Theme:** Survival, remembrance, testimony, record of what God kept, living witness
**Status:** Pending Loretta Approval

---

## Chapter Body

Some people write their story after they survive.

But sometimes, your survival is the story.

I look back over my life and I see more than events. I see evidence. Evidence that God kept me. Evidence that Heaven remembered me. Evidence that what I went through did not disappear just because other people did not see it.

My life became a scroll of survival.

Every tear God caught was written there.

Every prayer I prayed when I did not know what else to say was written there.

Every hospital room, every valley, every shadow, every closed door, every midnight, every report, every season where I had to keep breathing by faith — written there.

Malachi 3:16 says that a book of remembrance was written before Him.

That touches me because there were seasons when I felt forgotten by people, but I was never forgotten by God. He remembered what I carried. He remembered what I survived. He remembered the days I kept saying yes when my heart was tired.

People may forget the details.

People may minimize the pain.

People may never know the full cost.

But God remembers.

And if God remembers, then my testimony still matters.

The scroll of survival is not a scroll of shame. It is not a record of defeat. It is a witness that I lived through what tried to silence me. It is a record that says, "God was there."

I am not just telling what happened.

I am testifying to Who kept me.

I am a living testimony.

A living scroll.

A book of remembrance walking in flesh.

And every chapter of my survival points back to Him.

## Reader Takeaway
Your survival is not invisible to God. Heaven remembers what people forget, and your life can become a living testimony of His keeping power.

## Prayer
Lord, thank You for remembering what I survived. Thank You for keeping record when others forgot, misunderstood, or never saw the full cost. Let my life become a testimony that points back to You. Heal every place where I felt unseen. Help me honor my survival without shame. Let every reader know that Heaven remembers them, too. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
God remembers what I survived.
My life is a living testimony.
Nothing Heaven carried me through was wasted.
I am not forgotten.
My survival points back to God.

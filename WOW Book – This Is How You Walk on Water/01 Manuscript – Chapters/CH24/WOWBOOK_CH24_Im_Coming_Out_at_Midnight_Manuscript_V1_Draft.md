# CH24 – I'm Coming Out at Midnight

**Scripture:** Acts 16:25–26 — "And at midnight Paul and Silas prayed, and sang praises unto God… and immediately all the doors were opened…"
**Theme:** Midnight breakthrough, praise in pain, deliverance, closed doors opening, God moving in the darkest hour
**Status:** Approved (Loretta) — 2026-07-03

---

## Chapter Body

Midnight has a sound.

It is the sound of everything being quiet around you, but loud inside of you. It is the sound of your thoughts trying to race while your spirit is trying to hold on. It is the hour when pain feels heavier, questions feel deeper, and the enemy tries to convince you that morning is not coming.

But midnight is also where God can move.

Acts 16 says that at midnight, Paul and Silas prayed and sang praises unto God. They were not in a comfortable place. They were not in a place that looked like victory. They were bound, watched, and locked in.

But they still prayed.

They still sang.

And at midnight, God shook the prison.

That scripture speaks to me because I know what it feels like to have to praise from a place that does not look like freedom yet. I know what it feels like to pray when nothing has opened. I know what it feels like to worship while waiting for the doors to move.

But I also know this:

God can still shake what has been holding you.

The midnight hour does not mean God is late. Sometimes midnight becomes the place where breakthrough announces itself. Sometimes the darkest hour becomes the doorway of deliverance. Sometimes the place that looked locked becomes the place where God proves He still has the keys.

I am coming out at midnight.

Not because I never cried.

Not because the prison was not real.

Not because the chains did not hurt.

But because prayer still has power.

Praise still has sound.

God still shakes locked places.

And doors still open when Heaven speaks.

There were seasons where I did not know how I was coming out. I just knew I could not stop calling on God. I could not stop worshiping. I could not stop believing that the same God who opened prison doors before could still open what had been closed over me.

Midnight did not bury me.

Midnight became my breakthrough hour.

And when the shaking came, I knew God was in it.

## Reader Takeaway
Do not stop praying because it is midnight. God can use the darkest hour to open the doors that looked locked.

## Prayer
Lord, meet me in the midnight hour. Give me strength to pray when I am tired and praise when I still feel bound. Shake every prison that has held my mind, my heart, my body, my family, my purpose, or my promise. Open the doors that only You can open. Let every reader know that midnight is not too late for You to move. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
I am coming out at midnight.
Prayer still has power.
Praise still opens doors.
God can shake what has been holding me.
Midnight is not too late for breakthrough.

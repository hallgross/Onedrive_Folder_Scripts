# CH21 – The Tomb Has Been Rolled Away: I Will Rise

**Scripture:** Matthew 28:2 — "...for the angel of the Lord descended from heaven, and came and rolled back the stone from the door…"
**Theme:** Resurrection, rising again, freedom, deliverance, new life, God opening what was sealed
**Status:** Pending Loretta Approval

---

## Chapter Body

There are seasons that feel like a tomb.

Dark.

Silent.

Sealed.

Final.

You look around and wonder how you got there. You wonder how pain could close in like that. You wonder how grief, sickness, rejection, betrayal, and loss could pile up until it feels like a stone has been rolled in front of your life.

I know what it feels like to be in a place that looked final.

But God specializes in stones.

Matthew 28:2 says the angel of the Lord descended from heaven and rolled back the stone from the door.

That scripture reminds me that what people call final, God can still open. What pain seals, God can still break. What the enemy thinks is finished, Heaven can still interrupt.

The tomb was real.

But so was resurrection.

There were places in my life where I felt buried under circumstances. Buried under reports. Buried under grief. Buried under rejection. Buried under battles people did not even know I was fighting.

But I was not dead.

I was hidden.

I was healing.

I was being kept.

And when the time came, God rolled the stone away.

I had to learn that every closed place is not my ending. Some closed places are where God proves He still has power. Some silent places are where Heaven is working. Some buried seasons become resurrection testimonies.

The stone did not move because I was strong enough.

The stone moved because God sent help.

The tomb has been rolled away.

And I will rise.

Not because I was never wounded.

Not because I was never tired.

Not because the tomb was not real.

But because the God of resurrection still calls life out of sealed places.

I will rise from what tried to bury me.

I will rise from what tried to silence me.

I will rise from what tried to define me.

The tomb was not my home.

The stone was not my sentence.

God rolled it away.

## Reader Takeaway
What looks sealed is not always finished. God still knows how to roll stones away and call life out of buried places.

## Prayer
Lord, thank You for rolling away the stones that tried to seal my life. Thank You that what looked final was not final in Your hands. Call life into every buried place. Strengthen every reader who feels trapped behind a stone of grief, sickness, rejection, or fear. Let resurrection power enter the places that looked dead. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
The tomb is not my home.
The stone is not my sentence.
God is rolling away what tried to seal me.
I will rise by resurrection power.
What looked final is not final with God.

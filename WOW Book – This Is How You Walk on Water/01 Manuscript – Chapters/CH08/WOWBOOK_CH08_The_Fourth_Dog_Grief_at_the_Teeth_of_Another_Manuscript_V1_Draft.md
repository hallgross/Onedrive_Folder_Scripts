# CH08 – The Fourth Dog: Grief at the Teeth of Another

**Scripture:** Psalm 34:18 — "The Lord is nigh unto them that are of a broken heart; and saveth such as be of a contrite spirit."
**Theme:** Grief, loss, trauma, companionship, love, brokenheartedness, God's comfort
**Status:** Needs Loretta / My Grace Content Review

---

## Chapter Body

Some losses sound small to people who did not love what you loved.

But grief knows the truth.

When I lost my fourth dog, it was not just the loss of an animal. It was the loss of companionship, comfort, routine, and a little heartbeat that had been part of my life. It came in a way that hurt deeply, at the teeth of another dog, and it added another layer of pain to a season that already felt heavy.

There are some tears people understand.

And there are some tears you carry quietly.

This was one of those griefs that touched a tender place in me. When you have already walked through loss, betrayal, pressure, and uncertainty, another loss can feel like one more thing your heart has to survive.

But Psalm 34:18 says, "The Lord is nigh unto them that are of a broken heart…"

That means God does not only come near for the losses people consider important. He comes near to the brokenhearted. Period.

He comes near when your heart breaks over a person.

He comes near when your heart breaks over a home.

He comes near when your heart breaks over a child.

He comes near when your heart breaks over a little dog that brought comfort in a hard season.

God is not embarrassed by what hurts us.

He does not stand far away from grief because others do not understand it. He draws close. He sits with us in the silence. He holds the parts of our hearts that cannot explain why something hurt so deeply.

I will not make this chapter graphic. Love does not need blood to prove grief. The empty space was enough. The collar, the memory, the quiet, the missing presence — all of it spoke.

And God was there.

He was near to the brokenhearted.

He was near to me.

This chapter is not only about losing my fourth dog. It is about the God who comes close when another loss tries to break what is already wounded.

Even then, He kept my heart.

## Reader Takeaway
God cares about every grief that touches your heart, even the grief others may not understand. If it broke your heart, God came near.

## Prayer
Lord, thank You for being near to the brokenhearted. Thank You for caring about the losses others may not understand. Heal the grief that sits quietly in my heart. Comfort every tender place that has had to say goodbye. Remind me that You are not distant from pain, and You are not dismissive of what mattered to me. Let every reader know that You are near in every kind of loss. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
God is near to my broken heart.
My grief matters to God.
The Lord comforts every tender place in me.
Loss did not harden my heart.
God kept my heart even in grief.

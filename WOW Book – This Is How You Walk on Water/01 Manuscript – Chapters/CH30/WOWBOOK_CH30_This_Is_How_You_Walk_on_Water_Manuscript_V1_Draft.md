# CH30 – This Is How You Walk on Water

**Scripture:** Matthew 14:29 — "And he said, Come. And when Peter was come down out of the ship, he walked on the water, to go to Jesus."
**Theme:** Faith, obedience, stepping out, water testimony, surrender, knowing God through the storm
**Status:** Approved (Loretta) — 2026-07-03

---

## Chapter Body

This is how you walk on water.

You hear Him say, "Come."

And you step.

Not because the water looks safe.

Not because the wind is quiet.

Not because the storm has stopped.

Not because you understand everything.

You step because Jesus called you.

Matthew 14:29 says, "And he said, Come. And when Peter was come down out of the ship, he walked on the water, to go to Jesus."

That scripture carries my whole testimony.

Because I have known water.

Deep water.

Healing water.

Storm water.

Tears that felt like water.

Trials that moved like waves.

Seasons where the ground under me did not feel steady and I had to trust the voice of God more than what I could see.

Walking on water does not mean you never feel afraid.

It means you keep your eyes on the One who called you.

It means you step while the wind is still blowing.

It means you trust while the waves are still moving.

It means you learn that the water is not stronger than His Word.

When I look back over my life — foster care, loss, rejection, sickness, hospital rooms, rods, reports, shadows, grief, betrayal, delays, fire, and midnight seasons — I can see something now that I did not always understand while I was going through it.

God was teaching me to know Him.

And then I heard it with spiritual ears:

"You have been chosen to know Me."

That changed how I saw the sickness.

That changed how I saw the trials.

That changed how I saw the tribulations.

I was not just chosen to survive.

I was chosen to know Him.

To know Him as Healer.

To know Him as Keeper.

To know Him as Father.

To know Him as Comforter.

To know Him as Provider.

To know Him as Defender.

To know Him as the One who stayed.

To know Him in the hospital room.

To know Him in the water.

To know Him when the door closed.

To know Him when the shadow came.

To know Him when the fire tested me.

To know Him when the midnight hour felt long.

This is what the book is really about.

Not just what happened to me.

But who God became to me in it.

The water did not drown me because His Word was holding me.

The fire did not consume me because His presence was with me.

The grave did not keep me because His resurrection power called me out.

The shadow did not own me because I was under the shadow of the Almighty.

The report did not name me because God had already called me.

The door that rejected me did not cancel the nations He assigned to me.

So this is how you walk on water:

You listen for His voice.

You step out of what feels safe.

You keep your eyes on Jesus.

You let Him hold you when your faith trembles.

You get back up when you sink.

You let the water become your testimony.

And you never forget that the One who said "Come" is the same One who will reach for you.

I am still walking.

I am still healing.

I am still learning.

I am still saying yes.

This is how you walk on water.

## Reader Takeaway
Walking on water is not about never facing storms. It is about trusting the voice of Jesus enough to step, keep looking at Him, and know Him in the middle of the waves.

## Prayer
Lord Jesus, thank You for calling me out onto the water. Thank You for being with me in every storm, every trial, every sickness, every loss, and every wave. Teach me to keep my eyes on You when life feels unstable. Help me hear Your voice above the wind. Thank You that I was not just chosen to survive, but chosen to know You. Let every reader receive courage to step out, trust You, and walk by faith. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
I hear Jesus saying, “Come.”
I will walk by faith, not fear.
The water is not stronger than His Word.
I was not just chosen to survive; I was chosen to know Him.
This is how I walk on water.

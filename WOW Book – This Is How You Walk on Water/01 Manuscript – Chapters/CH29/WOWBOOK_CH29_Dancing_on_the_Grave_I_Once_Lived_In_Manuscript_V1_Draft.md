# CH29 – I'm Dancing on the Grave I Once Lived In

**Scripture:** Psalm 30:11 — "Thou hast turned for me my mourning into dancing…"
**Theme:** Victory, resurrection joy, freedom, celebration, no longer living in the old place, testimony after survival
**Status:** Approved (Loretta) — 2026-07-03

---

## Chapter Body

There are places I used to live that I no longer call home.

I used to live in fear.

I used to live in grief.

I used to live in rejection.

I used to live in survival mode.

I used to live under reports, shadows, wounds, and questions.

But God brought me out.

Psalm 30:11 says, "Thou hast turned for me my mourning into dancing…"

That scripture is not just poetry to me. It is testimony.

Because there was mourning.

There were tears.

There were losses.

There were nights I did not know how I was going to keep standing.

There were seasons that felt like they buried parts of me.

But then God started turning.

He turned my mourning.

He turned my pain.

He turned my shame.

He turned my silence.

He turned what tried to keep me down into a place where my feet could move again.

That is why I say, "I'm dancing on the grave I once lived in."

Not because the grave was not real.

Not because the pain did not happen.

Not because I forgot what it cost me.

But because I do not live there anymore.

The grave is not my address.

The wound is not my name.

The loss is not my identity.

The report is not my future.

What once held me has become the ground beneath my victory dance.

Every step I take is a declaration.

I survived this.

God kept me through this.

I am not buried here anymore.

There is a holy joy that comes after survival. It is not shallow joy. It is not pretend joy. It is the kind of joy that knows what sorrow felt like and still chooses to move.

It is the kind of dance that says, "The enemy did not win."

It is the kind of dance that says, "God brought me out."

It is the kind of dance that says, "I was once here, but I do not live here anymore."

I am dancing on the grave I once lived in.

And every step belongs to God.

## Reader Takeaway
You are not required to live forever in the place that wounded you. God can turn mourning into dancing and make the old grave become the ground of your victory.

## Prayer
Lord, thank You for turning my mourning into dancing. Thank You for bringing me out of places I thought I would never escape. Heal every place where pain tried to become my home. Teach me to celebrate without shame, to move again without fear, and to dance because You brought me out. Let every reader know they do not have to live in the grave of what happened to them. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
I do not live in the grave anymore.
God has turned my mourning into dancing.
What once held me is now under my feet.
My wound is not my name.
Every step of my victory belongs to God.

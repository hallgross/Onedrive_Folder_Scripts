# CH07 – Season of Shadows: When I Lost Everything, But God Kept Me

**Scripture:** Psalm 23:4 — "Yea, though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil: for thou art with me…"
**Theme:** Loss from every side; the valley of the shadow; God kept me
**Status:** Pending Loretta Approval

---

## Chapter Body

There was a season I call the season of shadows.

It was not one thing.

It was everything.

It felt like loss came from every direction at the same time. Benefits were lost. Betrayal came close. Children walked away. Money started acting funny. My car became another concern. Disconnection notices came. Grief came through the loss of my fourth dog. And after twenty-three years in my home, I found myself facing hardship in a way I had never had to face before.

I remember thinking, "Lord, what is happening?"

Because there are seasons where you can handle one storm. You can pray through one thing. You can stand through one disappointment. But when loss starts coming from every side, your soul begins to feel the weight of it.

That is why I call it shadows.

Not because God was gone, but because so much was pressing around me that I had to fight to see the light.

Psalm 23:4 says, "Yea, though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil: for thou art with me…"

I learned something in that valley.

A shadow can look bigger than what is really there.

A shadow can make you feel surrounded.

A shadow can make you think the thing has more power than it has.

But a shadow also proves there is light somewhere.

And even in that season, God was the light.

He did not let the shadows have the final word. He did not let the notices become my name. He did not let betrayal become my identity. He did not let loss bury my testimony. He did not let grief swallow me whole.

I had days when I did not know how everything was going to work out.

But God kept me.

I had moments when I looked around my home and could feel the pressure of twenty-three years meeting a season I never expected.

But God kept me.

I had tears that only Heaven saw.

But God kept me.

There are seasons when God does not just bless you with increase. Sometimes He blesses you by keeping your mind, keeping your faith, keeping your breath, keeping your house, keeping your yes, keeping your worship, and keeping you from falling apart when everything around you is shaking.

This chapter is my witness that the valley was real.

The shadow was real.

The loss was real.

But God was real too.

And He kept me.

## Reader Takeaway
A shadow can feel powerful, but it cannot exist without light. Even in seasons where everything seems to fall apart, God can still keep you.

## Prayer
Lord, thank You for keeping me in the season of shadows. Thank You for being present when pressure came from every side. Heal the places where betrayal, loss, grief, and fear tried to overwhelm me. Remind me that shadows are not greater than Your light. Strengthen every reader who is facing notices, loss, instability, grief, or uncertainty. Let them know that You are still with them in the valley. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
God kept me in the season of shadows.
The valley did not destroy me.
The shadow is not greater than the light.
Loss did not cancel my testimony.
God is with me, and I will not fear.

**Privacy Boundaries**
- Do not show readable bills, addresses, account numbers, benefit letters, disconnection notices, medical papers, or private documents.
- Do not name people connected to betrayal or children walking away.
- Do not make accusations.
- Do not make the image too dark or hopeless.
- Use symbols: unopened envelopes, keys, empty collar, dim home, golden light.
- Keep the tone honest, dignified, and hopeful.

**Visual Direction**
A graceful Black woman standing in her longtime home. Around her are symbolic signs of hardship: unopened envelopes with no readable text, car keys, an empty pet collar, shadows near the edges of the room. Golden light breaks through the window or doorway, showing God's presence. No readable private information.

**Video Direction**
Start with dim symbolic home details: keys, sealed envelopes, empty collar, a quiet room. Then show golden light entering. End with the woman standing still and breathing, not defeated.

# CH10 – I Stayed. They Strayed. But God Stayed With Me

**Scripture:** Galatians 6:9 — "And let us not be weary in well doing: for in due season we shall reap, if we faint not."
**Theme:** Faithfulness, endurance, disappointment, love, release, God's loyalty
**Status:** Needs Loretta / My Grace Review

---

## Chapter Body

There is a cost to staying.

People talk about leaving, but sometimes staying hurts too. Staying faithful. Staying prayerful. Staying loving. Staying available. Staying in position when your heart is tired and your hands feel empty.

I know what it feels like to stay.

I stayed in prayer. I stayed in love. I stayed believing. I stayed covering. I stayed when I did not always understand why things were going the way they were going.

And yes, some strayed.

Some walked in ways I could not control. Some made choices I could not carry for them. Some moved in directions that grieved me. Some forgot the love, the prayers, the covering, and the sacrifices that helped carry them.

But this chapter is not about exposing who strayed.

This chapter is about the God who stayed.

Galatians 6:9 says, "And let us not be weary in well doing: for in due season we shall reap, if we faint not."

That scripture means something different when you are tired from doing good.

It means something different when you have loved and still felt forgotten. It means something different when you have prayed and still watched distance come. It means something different when you have poured out and wondered if any of it mattered.

But God reminded me: do not grow weary.

Not because people always understand.

Not because everyone returns.

Not because the harvest comes when you want it.

But because God sees the seed.

He sees the prayers nobody clapped for. He sees the love that cost you. He sees the nights you kept going when your heart wanted to sit down. He sees the times you stayed faithful when disappointment tried to make you bitter.

I stayed.

They strayed.

But God stayed with me.

And sometimes that is the miracle — not that everything looks fixed, but that God keeps your heart from becoming hard while you wait. He keeps your hands clean. He keeps your spirit soft. He keeps your yes alive.

I am learning that staying does not mean carrying what only God can carry.

Staying does not mean losing myself in someone else's choices.

Staying does not mean I stop healing.

Staying means I remain faithful to God while I release what belongs in His hands.

I stayed in faith.

I stayed in prayer.

I stayed in love.

And when my heart was weary, God stayed with me.

## Reader Takeaway
Staying faithful does not mean carrying what belongs to God. You can love, pray, and release without becoming bitter.

## Prayer
Lord, strengthen me in the places where I am tired from doing good. Heal the disappointment that came from loving and not always being understood. Teach me how to stay faithful without carrying what only You can carry. Keep my heart soft, my hands clean, and my spirit free from bitterness. Remind me that You see every seed, every prayer, and every sacrifice. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
God sees every seed I have sown.
I will not grow weary in well doing.
I can love and release at the same time.
God stayed with me when others strayed.
My faithfulness is not wasted.

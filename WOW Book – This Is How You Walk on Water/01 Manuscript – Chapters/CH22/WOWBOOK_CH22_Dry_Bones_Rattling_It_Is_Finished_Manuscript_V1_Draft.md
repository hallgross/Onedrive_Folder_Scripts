# CH22 – Dry Bones Rattling: It Is Finished

**Scripture:** Ezekiel 37:4–5 — "Prophesy upon these bones, and say unto them, O ye dry bones, hear the word of the Lord…"
**Theme:** Restoration, resurrection, prophecy, dead places coming alive, God breathing again
**Status:** Approved (Loretta) — 2026-07-03

---

## Chapter Body

There are some seasons that look dry.

No movement.

No sound.

No breath.

No proof that anything is still alive.

I have known places like that. Places in my life that looked scattered. Places that looked too broken to come back together. Places where hope felt quiet, and the promise looked like it had been left in pieces.

But God knows how to speak in a valley.

In Ezekiel 37, God asked, "Can these bones live?" And then He told the prophet to speak to the bones.

That moves me because sometimes God will tell you to speak to what looks dead before you see any evidence of life.

You have to prophesy before the rattling starts.

You have to obey before the bones come together.

You have to believe before breath enters the room.

I have had to speak over dry places in my own life.

Over my body.

Over my mind.

Over my home.

Over my family.

Over my calling.

Over the dreams that looked delayed.

Over the promises that looked scattered.

And sometimes all I could say was, "Lord, breathe again."

Then comes the sound.

A rattling.

A shifting.

A coming together.

Something that looked finished starts responding to the Word of the Lord.

That is why I say, "It is finished," not because the enemy finished me, but because Jesus finished what needed to be finished. The power of death does not get the final word. The valley does not get the final word. Dry bones do not get the final word.

God does.

There are parts of my testimony that should not be standing.

But they are.

There are parts of my story that should have stayed scattered.

But God brought them together.

There are places where breath should have been gone.

But God breathed again.

This chapter is my witness that God can walk into a valley, speak to what looks dead, and make it live.

I heard dry bones rattling.

And I knew God was not finished with me.

## Reader Takeaway
Do not stop speaking life just because something looks dry. God can breathe again into what looks finished.

## Prayer
Lord, speak into every dry place in my life. Breathe over what looks scattered, delayed, broken, or impossible. Teach me to prophesy Your Word before I see movement. Let every dead place hear the voice of the Lord. Bring bones together, restore what was broken, and breathe Your life into me again. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
Dry bones will hear the Word of the Lord.
God is breathing again over my life.
What looked dead can live.
The valley is not my ending.
God is not finished with me.

God was in the valley. God was in the dry places. God was in the sound of rattling. God was in the breath coming back. God was proving that dead does not mean done when He is speaking.

- Keep the bones symbolic, biblical, and hopeful.
- Make the scene about restoration and breath, not death.

**Visual Direction**
A prophetic valley scene with dry bones coming together under golden heavenly breath, while a graceful Black woman stands in faith, speaking or praying. The image should feel biblical and powerful, but not frightening. Golden light should show God's breath restoring life.

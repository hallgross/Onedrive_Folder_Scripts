## Chapter Body

Some people see me standing and do not know what it costs my body to stand.

They do not know the rods. They do not know the bolts. They do not know the pain that has lived behind my smile. They do not know the nights my body talked louder than my mouth ever could.

But God knows.

I am still living with rods. I am still living with what my body has been through. I am still walking with evidence that something happened, something was repaired, something had to be held together.

And yet, I am still here.

Isaiah 53:5 says, "...and with his stripes we are healed."

That scripture means something different when your own body carries marks, metal, scars, pain, and memories of survival. Healing is not always the absence of evidence. Sometimes healing is the grace to keep standing with the evidence still in your body.

There was a time when the doctor told me my rods had shifted.

That kind of report can shake you.

Your mind starts trying to understand what that means. Your heart starts trying to stay calm. Your faith has to stand in the space between what was said and what God is still able to do.

But after more testing, the report was different.

The doctor said the rods were very well stable and did not appear to be going anywhere.

And I heard God in that.

What sounded frightening at first became another place where God reminded me He was still holding me together.

I was not just being held by metal.

I was being held by mercy.

I was not just standing because of rods.

I was standing because of grace.

There are things in my body that tell a story. There are scars that speak. There are places of pain that remind me I survived what was meant to take me out.

But I do not worship the pain.

I worship the God who kept me through it.

Still living with rods does not mean I am not healed. It means healing is still speaking through my life. It means God can be glorified even in a body that has been through fire, surgery, weakness, and waiting.

I am not ashamed of what helped hold me together.

But I know Who truly held me.

Thirty-nine bolts may tell part of the story.

But His stripes tell the greater one.

## Reader Takeaway
Healing does not always mean there is no evidence left behind. Sometimes healing is God giving you grace to stand with the evidence still in your body.

## Prayer
Lord, thank You for holding me together. Thank You for every place where my body survived what tried to break me. Thank You for being present in surgery, pain, testing, and recovery. Heal the fear that comes with medical reports. Strengthen every person living with scars, rods, bolts, pain, or hidden battles. Remind us that we are not only held by what doctors placed in our bodies — we are held by Your mercy. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
God is holding me together.
My scars do not cancel my healing.
My body carries evidence, but my spirit carries victory.
I am held by mercy, not just by metal.
By His stripes, I am healed.

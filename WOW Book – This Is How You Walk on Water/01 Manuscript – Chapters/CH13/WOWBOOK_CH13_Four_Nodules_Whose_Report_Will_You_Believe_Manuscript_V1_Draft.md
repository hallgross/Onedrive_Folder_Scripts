# CH13 – The Four Nodules Diagnosis: Whose Report Will You Believe?

**Scripture:** Isaiah 53:1 — "Who hath believed our report? and to whom is the arm of the Lord revealed?"
**Theme:** Diagnosis, fear, medical reports, faith, healing, choosing God's report
**Status:** Needs Loretta / My Grace Review

---

## Chapter Body

There are some words you hear from a doctor that make everything in you get quiet.

A diagnosis can do that.

It can enter a room and try to take up all the space. It can try to speak louder than your prayers, louder than your worship, louder than what God already told you about your life.

When I heard about the four nodules, my heart had to process it.

I had to sit with what was being said. I had to breathe through the thoughts that tried to come. I had to remind myself that a report may be real, but it is not greater than God.

Isaiah 53:1 says, "Who hath believed our report? and to whom is the arm of the Lord revealed?"

That scripture became a question my faith had to answer.

Whose report will you believe?

Will I believe the report that fear tried to write?

Will I believe the report that sickness tried to speak?

Will I believe the report that made my body feel uncertain?

Or will I believe the report of the Lord?

Believing God's report does not mean pretending I did not hear the doctor. It does not mean ignoring what needs attention. It does not mean acting like my body was not going through something.

It means I choose not to let the diagnosis become my identity.

It means I can listen to medical wisdom and still hold on to divine truth.

It means I can go through testing and still declare that God has the final word.

There is a battle that happens after a report.

A battle in the mind.

A battle in the emotions.

A battle in the spirit.

But I have learned that faith does not always shout first. Sometimes faith whispers from a shaking place, "Lord, I still believe You."

And that whisper matters.

I did not need to be fearless to have faith. I just needed to bring my fear under the feet of Jesus.

The four nodules were part of my journey, but they were not the author of my story.

The doctor had a report.

But God had a Word.

## Reader Takeaway
A diagnosis may be real, but it is not your identity. You can respect medical wisdom and still believe God's report over your life.

## Prayer
Lord, strengthen me when reports try to shake my faith. Help me hear Your voice above fear, sickness, and uncertainty. Thank You for doctors, testing, and wisdom, but thank You most of all that You have the final word over my life. Heal my body, calm my mind, and anchor my spirit in Your truth. Let every reader facing a diagnosis know that they are not alone and that Your arm is still revealed. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
I believe the report of the Lord.
A diagnosis is not my identity.
God has the final word over my body.
Fear will not write my testimony.
I can walk through testing and still trust God.

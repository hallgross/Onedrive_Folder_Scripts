# CH04 – Mother of Many Nations: Rejected at the Door, Called by God

**Scripture:** Isaiah 55:5 — "Behold, thou shalt call a nation that thou knowest not, and nations that knew not thee shall run unto thee because of the LORD thy God, and for the Holy One of Israel; for he hath glorified thee."
**Theme:** Rejection, mother wound, identity, nations, divine calling
**Status:** Needs Loretta Review

---

## Chapter Body

There are some pains you do not forget.

Not because you want to hold on to them, but because they touched a place so deep in your heart that only God could get down in there and heal it.

I know what it feels like to lose a mother.

I know what it feels like to carry that mother wound and still have to keep living, keep praying, keep showing up, keep being strong when a part of you still feels like a little girl needing to be held.

And I know what it feels like to travel far in hope.

I flew 3,000 miles.

I went with my heart open. I went believing. I went carrying love, expectation, questions, and maybe even a little girl inside of me still hoping for a door to open.

But I was turned away.

That kind of rejection does not just hurt your feelings. It tries to speak to your identity. It tries to tell you that you do not belong. It tries to make you question your worth. It tries to make you feel like the journey was for nothing.

But God.

That is the part the enemy never counts on.

Because while one door was closed to me, Heaven was still open over me.

Over twenty years ago — nearly twenty-five years ago — God gave me Isaiah 55:5:

"Behold, thou shalt call a nation that thou knowest not, and nations that knew not thee shall run unto thee because of the Lord thy God…"

At the time, I did not understand the fullness of it. I did not know how much pain I would walk through. I did not know how many times I would have to stand with tears in my eyes and still say yes to God. I did not know that the same woman who knew the ache of being turned away would one day hear God call her Mother of Many Nations.

But He knew.

God knew what He placed in me before rejection ever touched me.

God knew I would mother beyond blood.

God knew I would carry people in prayer.

God knew I would cover sons and daughters in the spirit.

God knew I would love people I did not birth, pray for nations I had not seen, and call forth destiny in people who did not even know my story.

So no, I am not Mother of Many Nations because my life was easy.

I am Mother of Many Nations because God named me before pain could bury me.

I am Mother of Many Nations because what people denied, God redeemed.

I am Mother of Many Nations because the door that rejected me was not strong enough to cancel the Word that called me.

I was turned away from a door.

But I was called into nations.

And that is how I know God was there.

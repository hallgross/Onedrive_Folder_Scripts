# CH20 – This Time the Enemy Will Not Steal

**Scripture:** John 10:10 — "The thief cometh not, but for to steal, and to kill, and to destroy: I am come that they might have life…"
**Theme:** Restoration, guarding the promise, spiritual authority, recovery, refusing theft, God's protection
**Status:** Approved (Loretta) — 2026-07-03

---

## Chapter Body

There comes a point where you recognize the pattern.

You look back and realize the enemy has been trying to steal from you for a long time.

Steal your peace.

Steal your joy.

Steal your health.

Steal your time.

Steal your relationships.

Steal your confidence.

Steal your voice.

Steal your promise.

And sometimes, if you have been through enough, you do not even notice at first that you have started expecting loss. You start bracing for another hit. You start preparing for another delay. You start wondering what else will be taken.

But then God wakes something up in you.

And you say, "This time, the enemy will not steal."

John 10:10 says, "The thief cometh not, but for to steal, and to kill, and to destroy: I am come that they might have life…"

That scripture tells me there is a thief, but it also tells me there is a Savior.

The thief came to steal.

But Jesus came to give life.

That means theft does not get to be the end of my story. Loss does not get to be the only pattern. Delay does not get to become my identity. What was attacked can still be restored by God.

I had to learn to guard what God gave me.

Guard my peace.

Guard my yes.

Guard my assignment.

Guard my healing.

Guard my mind.

Guard my worship.

Guard the promise that Heaven placed in my hands.

This chapter is not about fear of the enemy.

It is about authority in God.

It is about recognizing that what God gave me is worth protecting. It is about refusing to hand over my joy, my purpose, my voice, or my future just because warfare came against it.

The enemy may have stolen seasons.

But he will not steal the testimony.

He may have touched my body.

But he will not steal my worship.

He may have attacked my peace.

But he will not steal my promise.

This time, I am standing with my eyes open.

This time, I am guarding what God gave me.

This time, I am not just surviving the attack.

I am recovering what belongs to me.

## Reader Takeaway
You can recognize the thief without fearing him. What God gave you is worth guarding, and what was stolen can still be restored by God.

## Prayer
Lord, open my eyes to every place where the enemy has tried to steal from me. Restore my peace, my joy, my strength, my voice, my purpose, and my promise. Teach me to guard what You have placed in my life. I reject fear, loss, and agreement with theft. Thank You, Jesus, that You came so I may have life more abundantly. Let every reader recover what belongs to them in You. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration

Jesus came that I may have life.
I guard what God gave me.
What was stolen can be restored.
My testimony belongs to God.

# CH01 – In the Beginning: James and Margaret

**Scripture:** Jeremiah 1:5 — "Before I formed thee in the belly I knew thee…"
**Theme:** Origins & Foreknowledge
**Identity:** Known by God before the beginning

---

## Chapter Body

Before I knew my own name, God knew me.

Before I understood family, pain, rejection, love, loss, or purpose, Heaven had already written something over my life. Jeremiah 1:5 says, "Before I formed thee in the belly I knew thee…" That scripture tells me my beginning did not start with people's opinions, people's failures, or even people's ability to love me correctly. My beginning started in God.

James and Margaret are part of where my story begins. Their names are connected to my roots, my history, and the doorway of this testimony. This chapter is not about making everything perfect. It is about honoring the beginning and recognizing that even in the places I did not fully understand, God was already present.

There are parts of a life that only make sense when you look back and see the hand of God. Some things I survived before I had language for them. Some things I carried before I knew how heavy they were. But now I can look back and say, "Lord, You were there."

This is where the walk begins.

Not because everything was easy.

Not because every person stayed.

Not because every wound was explained.

But because before I was formed, God knew me. Before I walked through water, He had already called me to come forth. Before I became the testimony, I was already His.

## Reader Takeaway
Your life did not begin with pain.
It began with God's knowledge of you.

## Declaration
I was known by God before I was known by people.
My beginning belongs to God.
My roots are being healed by the Lord.
Nothing in my story was hidden from Heaven.
I am not an accident; I am called.

## Prayer
> _Draft from Loretta's Prayer Direction — approve or edit._

Father, thank You for knowing me before my beginning. Before I had a name, before I had breath, before anyone could love me or fail me, You already knew me and called me Yours. Thank You that my story did not start in pain — it started in You.

Lord, heal the roots of my story. Touch the places I carried before I had words for them, the things I survived before I understood them. Where there was loss, bring Your presence. Where there was rejection, remind me I was chosen. Help me look back and see Your hand in every place I did not fully understand, and say with peace, "Lord, You were there."

I thank You that I am not an accident. I am known, I am called, and my beginning belongs to You. In Jesus' name, Amen.

# CH02 – Every Girl Needs a James

**Scripture:** Proverbs 18:24 — "A man that hath friends must shew himself friendly: and there is a friend that sticketh closer than a brother."
**Theme:** Covering, protection, safe love, godly support

---

## Chapter Body

Every girl needs a James.

Every girl needs someone safe. Someone steady. Someone whose presence does not make her shrink, fear, or wonder if she matters. Every girl needs someone who can see the child inside of her and say, "You are worth protecting."

For me, James represents covering. Not perfection, not a perfect childhood, and not a life without pain — but covering. A reminder that even when a girl is moved from place to place, even when she does not have the photo albums, the family table, or the easy story, God can still place signs of His care along the way.

Being in foster care all my young life meant I did not carry the kind of childhood memories many people carry. I do not have boxes of pictures to prove every season. I do not have a neat album that explains where I was, who held me, or what I survived. But I do have a testimony.

And part of that testimony is this: God knew I needed covering.

A James is not just a name in this book. A James is a symbol of protection. A symbol of someone standing near enough to say, "You are not invisible." A symbol of godly strength that does not harm, but shelters. A symbol of love that does not take from a child, but guards what God placed inside of her.

There are some wounds only God can fully heal. There are some questions only Heaven can answer. But even in the places where my story was not easy, I can look back and see moments where God gave me signs that I was still seen.

Every girl needs a James.

Every girl needs covering.

And when people cannot cover you the way you need, God Himself becomes the covering that never fails.

## Declaration
I am worth protecting.
God is my covering.
My childhood wounds do not cancel my calling.
I am seen, known, and loved by God.
The Lord restores safety to the places that were wounded.

## Prayer
> _Optional draft for consistency with CH01 — approve, edit, or remove._

Father, thank You for being my covering when no one else could be. You saw the child in me and called her worth protecting. Where I was moved from place to place, You placed signs of Your care. Heal the wounds only You can heal, answer the questions only Heaven can answer, and restore safety to every place that was wounded. Thank You that my childhood wounds do not cancel my calling — I am seen, known, and loved by You. In Jesus' name, Amen.

## Short Caption
Every girl needs a James. Someone safe. Someone steady. Someone who reminds her she is worth protecting. And when life did not give me everything I needed, God still became my covering.

## Content Notes — In Loving Honor of James
James is no longer here in the earth, but what he represented still speaks. His life reminds Loretta that covering matters, safety matters, and honor matters. Even after his passing, his role in the story remains part of the testimony.

# CH03 – The Foster Room Wasn't Home, But God Was

**Scripture:** Psalm 27:10 — "When my father and my mother forsake me, then the Lord will take me up."
**Theme:** Foster care, belonging, God's presence when the room wasn't home

---

## Chapter Body

I do not have many pictures from my young life.

Foster care did not leave me with photo albums. It did not leave me with boxes full of childhood memories, birthday pictures, family vacations, or easy proof of where I belonged. There are parts of my early story that live more in my spirit than in photographs.

But just because there are no pictures does not mean God was not there.

There were rooms I stayed in that did not feel like home. There were places where my body was present, but my heart was still searching for belonging. A child can sit on a bed, look around a room, and know that the walls around her are not the same as being covered, chosen, or rooted.

But Psalm 27:10 says, "When my father and my mother forsake me, then the Lord will take me up."

That scripture became more than words. It became a witness.

God saw the little girl in the room. He saw what I could not explain. He saw the questions I did not have language for. He saw the loneliness, the moving, the waiting, the wondering, and the quiet places where I needed someone to say, "You belong."

And even when the room was not home, God was there.

I may not have all the childhood pictures, but I have testimony. I may not have albums from every season, but I have evidence in my spirit that the Lord kept me. He took me up when life did not hold me the way I needed to be held.

This chapter is not written to make my childhood look easy.

It is written to say that God was present in the rooms that did not feel like home.

## Reader Takeaway
You do not need photographs to prove you were kept. Even in the rooms that did not feel like home, God was present, and He took you up.

## Prayer
Lord, thank You for being with me in every room that did not feel like home. Thank You for seeing the child no one could fully hold, and taking me up. Heal the places that still search for belonging, and remind me that I am covered, chosen, and rooted in You. Let every reader who has felt unseen know that You were there all along. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
God was present in the rooms that did not feel like home.
The Lord took me up when life did not hold me.
I am covered, chosen, and rooted in God.
My testimony is my evidence.
I belong to God.

# CH04 – Mother of Many Nations: Rejected at the Door, Called by God

**Scripture:** Isaiah 55:5 — "Behold, thou shalt call a nation that thou knowest not, and nations that knew not thee shall run unto thee because of the LORD thy God, and for the Holy One of Israel; for he hath glorified thee."
**Theme:** Rejection, mother wound, identity, nations, divine calling

---

## Chapter Body

There are some pains you do not forget.

Not because you want to hold on to them, but because they touched a place so deep in your heart that only God could get down in there and heal it.

I know what it feels like to lose a mother.

I know what it feels like to carry that mother wound and still have to keep living, keep praying, keep showing up, keep being strong when a part of you still feels like a little girl needing to be held.

And I know what it feels like to travel far in hope.

I flew 3,000 miles.

I went with my heart open. I went believing. I went carrying love, expectation, questions, and maybe even a little girl inside of me still hoping for a door to open.

But I was turned away.

That kind of rejection does not just hurt your feelings. It tries to speak to your identity. It tries to tell you that you do not belong. It tries to make you question your worth. It tries to make you feel like the journey was for nothing.

But God.

That is the part the enemy never counts on.

Because while one door was closed to me, Heaven was still open over me.

Over twenty years ago — nearly twenty-five years ago — God gave me Isaiah 55:5:

"Behold, thou shalt call a nation that thou knowest not, and nations that knew not thee shall run unto thee because of the Lord thy God…"

At the time, I did not understand the fullness of it. I did not know how much pain I would walk through. I did not know how many times I would have to stand with tears in my eyes and still say yes to God. I did not know that the same woman who knew the ache of being turned away would one day hear God call her Mother of Many Nations.

But He knew.

God knew what He placed in me before rejection ever touched me.

God knew I would mother beyond blood.

God knew I would carry people in prayer.

God knew I would cover sons and daughters in the spirit.

God knew I would love people I did not birth, pray for nations I had not seen, and call forth destiny in people who did not even know my story.

So no, I am not Mother of Many Nations because my life was easy.

I am Mother of Many Nations because God named me before pain could bury me.

I am Mother of Many Nations because what people denied, God redeemed.

I am Mother of Many Nations because the door that rejected me was not strong enough to cancel the Word that called me.

I was turned away from a door.

But I was called into nations.

And that is how I know God was there.

## Reader Takeaway
A closed door cannot cancel the word God spoke over you. What people denied, God can redeem — and what rejected you cannot undo what He called you to.

## Prayer
Lord, thank You for naming me before pain could bury me. Heal the mother wound and the ache of being turned away. Remind me that while one door closed, Heaven stayed open over me. Help me carry people in prayer, cover sons and daughters in the spirit, and walk in the calling You placed in me before rejection ever touched me. Let every reader who was turned away know they are still called. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
God named me before pain could bury me.
What people denied, God redeemed.
The closed door could not cancel the Word that called me.
I was turned away from a door, but I was called into nations.
I am Mother of Many Nations.

# CH05 – The Footprint of Jesus in My Heart

**Scripture:** Psalm 119:105 — "Thy word is a lamp unto my feet, and a light unto my path."
**Theme:** The imprint of Jesus; His Word as lamp and light; presence in dark places, healing

---

## Chapter Body

There are some marks pain leaves.

But there are also marks Jesus leaves.

And the mark Jesus left in me is greater than every wound that tried to name me.

I call it the footprint of Jesus in my heart because there were places in my life where I know He walked with me. I may not have had all the answers. I may not have understood every season. I may not have known why some doors closed, why some people walked away, or why some roads were so hard.

But I know He was there.

Psalm 119:105 says, "Thy word is a lamp unto my feet, and a light unto my path." That scripture means something different when you have had to walk through dark places. It is one thing to quote it when everything is easy. It is another thing to live it when you cannot see the next step.

There were times when all I had was enough light for one step.

Not the whole road.

Not the whole answer.

Not the full explanation.

Just one step.

And Jesus was in that step.

His Word became the lamp. His presence became the light. His love became the imprint. Every time I thought I was walking alone, later I could look back and see that He had already passed through that place with me.

That is why I am still here.

Not because I was never wounded.

Not because I never cried.

Not because I always felt strong.

But because Jesus left His footprint in my heart, and that footprint became proof that I was not abandoned.

Some people have pictures. Some people have records. Some people have witnesses who can tell their story.

I have something deeper.

I have the mark of Jesus.

And His footprint in my heart keeps reminding me: "Keep walking. I am with you. I know the way."

## Reader Takeaway
Jesus can leave a holy imprint in places where pain tried to leave a scar. Even when you cannot see the whole path, His Word can give enough light for the next step.

## Prayer
Lord Jesus, thank You for walking with me through every season I did not understand. Thank You for leaving Your imprint on my heart. Heal the places where pain tried to mark me deeper than Your love. Let Your Word be a lamp to my feet and a light to my path. When I cannot see the whole road, give me grace for the next step. Let every reader know that You are still walking with them. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
Jesus has marked my heart with His love.
His Word is lighting my path.
I am not walking alone.
Pain did not leave the final imprint on me.
The footprint of Jesus is proof that He was there.

# CH06 – When Everyone Walked Out, God Walked In

**Scripture:** Psalm 27:10 — "When my father and my mother forsake me, then the Lord will take me up."
**Theme:** Abandonment healed; God's presence fills the empty room; the Lord takes me up

---

## Chapter Body

There is a kind of silence that comes after people leave.

It is not just quiet in the room. It is quiet in your heart. It is the kind of silence that makes you look around and realize the people you thought would stay are no longer standing where you thought they would be.

I know what that feels like.

I know what it feels like to be present for others, to pray, to cover, to love, to give, to believe, and then still feel the ache of being left. I know what it feels like when voices get quiet, doors close, phones stop ringing, and your heart has to learn how to breathe in a room that suddenly feels empty.

But God.

When everyone walked out, God walked in.

He did not always come with noise. He did not always come with an explanation. He did not always tell me why people left or why some seasons hurt the way they did. But He came with presence.

And sometimes presence is the miracle.

Psalm 27:10 says, "When my father and my mother forsake me, then the Lord will take me up." That scripture is not just about parents. It is about every place where human covering fails. It is about every moment where you feel dropped, dismissed, forgotten, or left standing alone.

The Lord will take me up.

That means God knows how to lift what people left. He knows how to hold what others mishandled. He knows how to step into empty spaces and fill them with glory.

I used to think being alone meant I had been abandoned.

But God taught me that sometimes an empty room becomes the place where His presence becomes undeniable.

When people walked out, I had to learn that their absence was not stronger than God's presence. Their silence was not louder than His voice. Their leaving was not the end of my story.

God walked in.

He walked into my grief.

He walked into my questions.

He walked into the rooms where I felt forgotten.

He walked into the places where I had no one to explain it to.

And He reminded me that I was still covered.

People may leave a room, but God can fill it. People may walk away, but God can walk closer. People may forget what you carried, but Heaven remembers every tear, every prayer, every sacrifice, and every yes.

This chapter is not about blaming the ones who left.

This chapter is about honoring the One who stayed.

## Reader Takeaway
People leaving does not mean God has left. Sometimes the empty room becomes the place where God's presence becomes the clearest.

## Prayer
Lord, heal the places in me that felt abandoned. Heal the silence people left behind. Heal the ache of being misunderstood, forgotten, or unsupported. Thank You for walking into the room when others walked out. Thank You for being the presence that stayed. Teach me not to measure my worth by who left, but by the God who never did. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
God stayed when people left.
I am not abandoned.
The empty room can still be filled with glory.
God's presence is stronger than people's absence.
The Lord will take me up.

# CH07 – Season of Shadows: When I Lost Everything, But God Kept Me

**Scripture:** Psalm 23:4 — "Yea, though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil: for thou art with me…"
**Theme:** Loss from every side; the valley of the shadow; God kept me

---

## Chapter Body

There was a season I call the season of shadows.

It was not one thing.

It was everything.

It felt like loss came from every direction at the same time. Benefits were lost. Betrayal came close. Children walked away. Money started acting funny. My car became another concern. Disconnection notices came. Grief came through the loss of my fourth dog. And after twenty-three years in my home, I found myself facing hardship in a way I had never had to face before.

I remember thinking, "Lord, what is happening?"

Because there are seasons where you can handle one storm. You can pray through one thing. You can stand through one disappointment. But when loss starts coming from every side, your soul begins to feel the weight of it.

That is why I call it shadows.

Not because God was gone, but because so much was pressing around me that I had to fight to see the light.

Psalm 23:4 says, "Yea, though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil: for thou art with me…"

I learned something in that valley.

A shadow can look bigger than what is really there.

A shadow can make you feel surrounded.

A shadow can make you think the thing has more power than it has.

But a shadow also proves there is light somewhere.

And even in that season, God was the light.

He did not let the shadows have the final word. He did not let the notices become my name. He did not let betrayal become my identity. He did not let loss bury my testimony. He did not let grief swallow me whole.

I had days when I did not know how everything was going to work out.

But God kept me.

I had moments when I looked around my home and could feel the pressure of twenty-three years meeting a season I never expected.

But God kept me.

I had tears that only Heaven saw.

But God kept me.

There are seasons when God does not just bless you with increase. Sometimes He blesses you by keeping your mind, keeping your faith, keeping your breath, keeping your house, keeping your yes, keeping your worship, and keeping you from falling apart when everything around you is shaking.

This chapter is my witness that the valley was real.

The shadow was real.

The loss was real.

But God was real too.

And He kept me.

## Reader Takeaway
A shadow can feel powerful, but it cannot exist without light. Even in seasons where everything seems to fall apart, God can still keep you.

## Prayer
Lord, thank You for keeping me in the season of shadows. Thank You for being present when pressure came from every side. Heal the places where betrayal, loss, grief, and fear tried to overwhelm me. Remind me that shadows are not greater than Your light. Strengthen every reader who is facing notices, loss, instability, grief, or uncertainty. Let them know that You are still with them in the valley. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
God kept me in the season of shadows.
The valley did not destroy me.
The shadow is not greater than the light.
Loss did not cancel my testimony.
God is with me, and I will not fear.

# CH08 – The Fourth Dog: Grief at the Teeth of Another

**Scripture:** Psalm 34:18 — "The Lord is nigh unto them that are of a broken heart; and saveth such as be of a contrite spirit."
**Theme:** Grief, loss, trauma, companionship, love, brokenheartedness, God's comfort

---

## Chapter Body

Some losses sound small to people who did not love what you loved.

But grief knows the truth.

When I lost my fourth dog, it was not just the loss of an animal. It was the loss of companionship, comfort, routine, and a little heartbeat that had been part of my life. It came in a way that hurt deeply, at the teeth of another dog, and it added another layer of pain to a season that already felt heavy.

There are some tears people understand.

And there are some tears you carry quietly.

This was one of those griefs that touched a tender place in me. When you have already walked through loss, betrayal, pressure, and uncertainty, another loss can feel like one more thing your heart has to survive.

But Psalm 34:18 says, "The Lord is nigh unto them that are of a broken heart…"

That means God does not only come near for the losses people consider important. He comes near to the brokenhearted. Period.

He comes near when your heart breaks over a person.

He comes near when your heart breaks over a home.

He comes near when your heart breaks over a child.

He comes near when your heart breaks over a little dog that brought comfort in a hard season.

God is not embarrassed by what hurts us.

He does not stand far away from grief because others do not understand it. He draws close. He sits with us in the silence. He holds the parts of our hearts that cannot explain why something hurt so deeply.

I will not make this chapter graphic. Love does not need blood to prove grief. The empty space was enough. The collar, the memory, the quiet, the missing presence — all of it spoke.

And God was there.

He was near to the brokenhearted.

He was near to me.

This chapter is not only about losing my fourth dog. It is about the God who comes close when another loss tries to break what is already wounded.

Even then, He kept my heart.

## Reader Takeaway
God cares about every grief that touches your heart, even the grief others may not understand. If it broke your heart, God came near.

## Prayer
Lord, thank You for being near to the brokenhearted. Thank You for caring about the losses others may not understand. Heal the grief that sits quietly in my heart. Comfort every tender place that has had to say goodbye. Remind me that You are not distant from pain, and You are not dismissive of what mattered to me. Let every reader know that You are near in every kind of loss. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
God is near to my broken heart.
My grief matters to God.
The Lord comforts every tender place in me.
Loss did not harden my heart.
God kept my heart even in grief.

# CH09 – The Daughter I Raised, the Silence I Carry

**Scripture:** Isaiah 49:15 — "Can a woman forget her sucking child… yea, they may forget, yet will I not forget thee."
**Theme:** A mother's silent love; release not rejection; God remembers every seed and prayer

---

## Chapter Body

There are some silences only a mother's heart understands.

It is one thing to love someone when everything is easy. It is another thing to love, raise, cover, pray, pour, sacrifice, and then still have to carry silence.

I have known that kind of ache.

The kind that sits quietly in your chest.

The kind you do not always explain because people may not understand the history, the cost, the love, or the years that went into it. The kind of pain that does not always need to be spoken loudly because God already heard it when it was still a whisper in your heart.

This chapter is not written to expose.

It is written to release.

Because a mother-heart can carry so much. It can carry memories. It can carry prayers. It can carry disappointments. It can carry questions. It can carry love even when love is not returned the way it was given.

Isaiah 49:15 says, "Can a woman forget her sucking child… yea, they may forget, yet will I not forget thee."

That scripture tells me something about God's memory.

People may forget what you did.

People may forget how you showed up.

People may forget the nights you prayed, the sacrifices you made, the covering you gave, and the love you carried.

But God does not forget.

He saw every seed.

He heard every prayer.

He knows what it cost you to love.

And He knows the difference between release and rejection.

There came a place where I had to put what I could not fix into God's hands. Not because I stopped loving. Not because it stopped mattering. Not because the silence did not hurt. But because I had to trust that God could hold what my heart could not keep carrying the same way.

Sometimes love has to pray from a distance.

Sometimes a mother-heart has to bless and release.

Sometimes you have to stop rehearsing the silence and start remembering that God is still speaking.

I carried love.

I carried pain.

I carried prayers.

But I did not carry them alone.

God was there in the silence too.

## Reader Takeaway
Love can be real even when there is distance. God remembers every seed, every prayer, and every sacrifice that people may forget.

## Prayer
Lord, heal the silent places in my heart. Heal the ache of love that was poured out and not fully returned. Teach me how to release what I cannot control without becoming bitter. Bless those I love, even when there is distance. Keep my heart tender, but protected. Remind me that You remember every seed, every sacrifice, and every prayer. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
God remembers what people forget.
My love was not wasted.
I release what I cannot control into God's hands.
Distance will not make me bitter.
God is healing the silence I carry.

# CH10 – I Stayed. They Strayed. But God Stayed With Me

**Scripture:** Galatians 6:9 — "And let us not be weary in well doing: for in due season we shall reap, if we faint not."
**Theme:** Faithfulness, endurance, disappointment, love, release, God's loyalty

---

## Chapter Body

There is a cost to staying.

People talk about leaving, but sometimes staying hurts too. Staying faithful. Staying prayerful. Staying loving. Staying available. Staying in position when your heart is tired and your hands feel empty.

I know what it feels like to stay.

I stayed in prayer. I stayed in love. I stayed believing. I stayed covering. I stayed when I did not always understand why things were going the way they were going.

And yes, some strayed.

Some walked in ways I could not control. Some made choices I could not carry for them. Some moved in directions that grieved me. Some forgot the love, the prayers, the covering, and the sacrifices that helped carry them.

But this chapter is not about exposing who strayed.

This chapter is about the God who stayed.

Galatians 6:9 says, "And let us not be weary in well doing: for in due season we shall reap, if we faint not."

That scripture means something different when you are tired from doing good.

It means something different when you have loved and still felt forgotten. It means something different when you have prayed and still watched distance come. It means something different when you have poured out and wondered if any of it mattered.

But God reminded me: do not grow weary.

Not because people always understand.

Not because everyone returns.

Not because the harvest comes when you want it.

But because God sees the seed.

He sees the prayers nobody clapped for. He sees the love that cost you. He sees the nights you kept going when your heart wanted to sit down. He sees the times you stayed faithful when disappointment tried to make you bitter.

I stayed.

They strayed.

But God stayed with me.

And sometimes that is the miracle — not that everything looks fixed, but that God keeps your heart from becoming hard while you wait. He keeps your hands clean. He keeps your spirit soft. He keeps your yes alive.

I am learning that staying does not mean carrying what only God can carry.

Staying does not mean losing myself in someone else's choices.

Staying does not mean I stop healing.

Staying means I remain faithful to God while I release what belongs in His hands.

I stayed in faith.

I stayed in prayer.

I stayed in love.

And when my heart was weary, God stayed with me.

## Reader Takeaway
Staying faithful does not mean carrying what belongs to God. You can love, pray, and release without becoming bitter.

## Prayer
Lord, strengthen me in the places where I am tired from doing good. Heal the disappointment that came from loving and not always being understood. Teach me how to stay faithful without carrying what only You can carry. Keep my heart soft, my hands clean, and my spirit free from bitterness. Remind me that You see every seed, every prayer, and every sacrifice. In Jesus' name, amen.

## Declaration
God sees every seed I have sown.
I will not grow weary in well doing.
I can love and release at the same time.
God stayed with me when others strayed.
My faithfulness is not wasted.

# CH11 – The Hospital Stay: God Was in the Room

**Scripture:** Psalm 46:1 — "God is our refuge and strength, a very present help in trouble."
**Theme:** Hospital stay; God present in weakness; healing beyond the physical

---

## Chapter Body

There are some places you do not forget.

A hospital room is one of them.

The sounds. The lights. The bed. The waiting. The way your body feels when it is tired, and the way your spirit has to keep reaching for God even when your strength feels low.

I know what it feels like to be in a hospital bed and still have to believe God.

I know what it feels like to look around a room and realize that healing is not just physical. Sometimes healing is emotional. Sometimes it is spiritual. Sometimes it is the grace to breathe through another night, trust through another test, and believe through another report.

But God was in the room.

He was not far from me because I was weak. He did not step away because my body was going through something. He did not measure me by tubes, tests, pain, or what the doctors were watching.

Psalm 46:1 says, "God is our refuge and strength, a very present help in trouble."

Very present.

That means He was not absent. He was not late. He was not standing outside the door waiting for me to get strong enough to deserve Him.

He was present in the trouble.

He was present in the weakness.

He was present in the room.

There were moments when I did not have a long prayer. Sometimes all I had was, "Lord, help me." Sometimes all I had was a tear. Sometimes all I had was enough faith to look up.
